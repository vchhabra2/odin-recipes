Miso Noodle Soup in a Jar

Description

Miso Soup is a classic Japanese soup and just as classic Japanese-American staple. This recipe of miso soup in a jar is inspired by minimalism and innovation.

Miso Soup is known for it's comfort and warmth of character. It pairs especially well with sushi.

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces extra-firm tofu
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup sliced green onions
  • 3 tablespoons white miso paste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon vegetable stock base
  • 1 tablespoon grated fresh ginger
  • 1 1/2 cups cooked rice vermicelli noodles
  • 1 cup thinly sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 1 cup thinly sliced baby bok choy

Steps

  1. Cut tofu into 1/2-inch planks.
  2. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Cook tofu, turning halfway through, until golden, 6 to 8 minutes.
  4. Cut tofu into cubes.
  5. Stir together green onions, miso paste, garlic, stock base, and ginger in a small bowl.
  6. Divide evenly among 3 microwave-safe pint-size canning jars with lids.
  7. Layer evenly with rice vermicelli noodles, tofu, mushrooms, and bok choy. Chill, covered, up to 5 days.
  8. To serve, see “Heat and Eat”. Garnish with sliced green onions.
